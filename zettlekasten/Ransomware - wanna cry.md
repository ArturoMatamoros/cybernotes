# What is a ransomware? 
A [[Ransomware]] is a type of Malware that hurts end users and business by using encryption to block all the data in the computer and asking for a ransome to get it back. Every malware needs a method of trasmission, one of them is by automatically sending itself through email. A malware that spreads like this is what we call a worm. 

# Why was WannaCry so effective? 
WannaCry was a ransomware [[Worm]], one of the only ones since usually ransomware are not spread through email. The worm they used was particularly powerful since it used a vulnerability that was completely uknown at the time. 
The Ransomware impacted more than 200.000 computers and major companies like FedEx, Honda, Nissan, and the UK's national health service. The ransomware was quickly neutralized, at least momentarily, but many computers never recovered their data. 

# What is a [[Zero-day exploit]]?
A zero-day is a vulnerability that is uknown to the developers and they are the most dangerous since they can be attacked without nobody even looking for them. The zero-day that they used is called EternalBlue and the group that made it public was Shadow brokers. 
We suspect that the government of noth korea was behind it but there are many theories about it. What we know is that this zero day was used to create the Ransomware. 

# How was the attack stopped?
The day of the attack a cybersecurity expert, passionate about malware, use the tecnique of reverse engineering, where you analize the code so you can understand the logic behind it, and found a query that brought to a website that did not exist. With this line he just registered the domain of the website and by doing that ruining the query and stopping the Ransomware.
